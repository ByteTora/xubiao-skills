摘要

目的：细胞周期蛋白依赖性激酶（CDKs）是一种控制细胞周期调节的关键蛋白，使CDK4/6成为抗乳腺癌治疗的一个有吸引力的靶点。然而，临床应用中的耐药性和其他副作用对CDK4/6抑制剂的结构提出了新的要求。本实验拟采用深度学习的方法，快速鉴定具有新骨架的CDK4/6抑制剂。
方法：通过建立深度强化学习模型来生成全新的候选小分子化合物库，深度强化学习模型包括生成模型（GENERATOR）和预测模型(PREDICTOR)，生成模型主要建立和优化预训练模型（Pre-training）和微调模型(Fine-tune)，其中微调模型的训练表征了小分子的骨架信息；预测模型主要建立基于支持向量分类（SVC）、随机森林（RF）、朴素贝叶斯（NB）、K-近邻（KNN）和多层感知机（MLP）的QSAR模型来预测生成小分子的活性。然后再通过类药性判断、骨架聚类、激酶选择性虚拟筛选等方法筛选出对CDK4/6靶点具有活性的先导化合物，然后再通过分子动力学模拟选择其中与目标蛋白结合稳定、亲和力强的苗头化合物；然后设计并合成了目标化合物，并对合成的目标化合物进行体外活性活性研究；最后，通过大鼠模型对代表化合的肿瘤抑制作用进行了体内评价。
结果：1、生成模型：经过CheMBL数据集训练的预训练模型表现出良好的性能，已经学习到SMILES语法的相关信息，因此被用作生成模型进行进一步微调；使用SMILES 枚举处理后的CDK4/6数据集对预训练模型进行微调，生成了类CDK4/6小分子，因此被选作GENERATOR。2、预测模型：RF取得了最高的马修斯相关系数（Matthews Correlation Coefficient，MCC）、准确率（Accuracy）、敏感度（Sensitivity）和特异度（Specificity），因此被选来作为PREDICTOR。3、强化学习模型生成了基于骨架的小分子库，共10000个小分子。经过虚拟筛选后，我们选择了两个新的化合物5和 12进行合成和活性评价，它们显示了有效的CDK4/6抑制谱（IC50分别为7.1 nM和17.2 nM）和对CDK2出色的选择性。化合物5有效地抑制了乳腺癌细胞中Rb蛋白的磷酸化，并在乳腺癌小鼠模型中显示出良好的口服治疗效果。
结论：通过深度强化学习方法建立的分子生成模型能够生成全新的基于骨架的小分子化合物，并表现出良好的肿瘤抑制效果，说明了深度学习方法在全新小分子药物设计中的可行性，对进一步开发CDK4/6抑制剂具有很大的参考价值。
关键词：CDK4/6；分子生成；深度强化学习；虚拟筛选
 
Abstract
Objective: Cell Cyclin-dependent kinase (CDKs) is a key protein that controls cell cycle regulation, making CDK4/6 an attractive target for anti-breast cancer therapy. However, drug resistance and other side effects in clinical applications have placed new demands on the structure of CDK4/6 inhibitors. In this experiment, we propose to use a deep learning approach to rapidly identify CDK4/6 inhibitors with novel backbones.
Methods: A deep reinforcement learning model is built to generate a novel library of candidate small molecule compounds. The deep reinforcement learning model includes a generative model (GENERATOR) and a predictive model (PREDICTOR), and the generative model mainly builds and optimizes a pre-training model (Pre-training) and a fine-tuning model (Fine-tune), where the training of the fine-tuning model characterizes the skeleton information of small molecules; the prediction model mainly builds QSAR models based on Support Vector Classification (SVC), Random Forest (RF), Parsimonious Bayes (NB), K-Nearest Neighbor (KNN) and Multilayer Perceptron (MLP) to predict the activity of generated small molecules. Then the lead compounds with activity against CDK4/6 targets were screened by drug-like judgment, backbone clustering, and kinase selective virtual screening, and then the seedling compounds among them with stable binding and strong affinity to the target proteins were selected by molecular dynamics simulation; then the target compounds were designed and synthesized, and the synthesized target compounds were investigated for in vitro activity; finally, the rat model for in vivo evaluation of the tumor suppressive effect of the representative chemistry.
Results: 1. Generative model: The pre-trained model trained with the ChEMBL dataset showed good performance and had learned relevant information about the SMILES syntax, and thus was used as a generative model for further fine-tuning; the pre-trained model was fine-tuned using the SMILES enumeration-processed CDK4/6 dataset, which generated CDK4/6-like small molecules, and thus was selected as GENERATOR. 2. Prediction model: RF achieved the highest Matthews Correlation Coefficient (MCC), Accuracy, Sensitivity and Specificity, and was therefore selected as PREDICTOR.3. The reinforcement learning model generated a skeleton-based small molecule library of 10,000 small molecules. After virtual screening, we selected two new compounds 5 and 12 for synthesis and activity evaluation, which showed effective CDK4/6 inhibition profiles (IC50 of 7.1 nM and 17.2 nM, respectively) and excellent selectivity for CDK2. Compound 5 effectively inhibited the phosphorylation of Rb protein in breast cancer cells and showed good oral therapeutic efficacy in a mouse model of breast cancer.
Conclusion: The molecular generation model established by deep reinforcement learning approach was able to generate novel backbone based small molecule compounds and showed good tumor suppression effects, illustrating the feasibility of deep learning approach in novel small molecule drug design, which is of great reference value for further development of CDK4/6 inhibitors.
Keywords: CDK4/6; molecule generation; deep reinforcement learning; virtual screening
